Analysing images of the James Webb Telescope

Nasa has Released Many Intricate Images from the James Webb Telescope Something you may not have known though, is that the data to said images is completely public. You do not need a great deal of experience to create a wonderful image, all you need is

  • A large amount of spare storage on your computer
  • Internet access, stable enough wifi to download large files
  • A bit of time to adjust the image to your preference

You can create many interesting variations based on the wavelength, distance, and many other factors

With a bit of fiddling, you can create an image that expresses the closer regions of the spiral galaxy

    Some interesting facts about the James Webb Telescope you may not know
  • Despite being launched only about 2 years ago, the Telescope has taken over a million photos, with about 60,000 of which being NIRCAM images
  • Compared to the Hubble space telescope, JWST will focus mainly on infared light, allowing it to see farther in the past
  • While the surface area of the gold plated mirrors is about 25 square meters, the thickness is a mere 100 nanometers making the total gold content the size of a golf ball
